The Client : UA College of Engineering

Tech Used : Wordpress / PHP / Photoshop

/01 Project context

  • Brand Strategy
  • Interface Design
  • Front End Development

As one of the premier engineering schools in the United States, the University of Arizona (UA) attracts prospective students from across the country and around the globe. Following a recent schoolwide rebrand, UA's College of Engineering needed to overhaul the design of its existing website in order to reflect a new set of branding guidelines.

I used this as an opportunity to refresh the aesthetics of their web presence and improve their noticeably cumbersome online admissions process.

/02 Initial project goals

1

Explore the use of graphic design assets and templates as laid out in the updated UA brand guidelines

2

Find a helpful tool that would assist the sales team in quickly connecting with leads

3

Redesign of all websites in order to accommodate and promote a growing number of graduate degree programs

4

Improve their online application process, particularly for international students

/03 The making of

Establishing a successful design system.

I worked closely with members of UA's marketing team to chose which updates were to take priority during the first phase of the relaunch. We focused on building an updated color palette and type stack, as well as developing a new lead capture process, as these would make the biggest impact upfront and help represent the College of Engineering within the larger UA brand.

Evolution of a component library

Drawing inspiration from the natural landscapes sourroundg its campus, the The UA brand was built from the ground up with the natural landscape surounding their campus as its focus. The stacked 'A' logomark and use of triangular shapes draws inspiration from a mountainanoue terrain and became the primary symbols for UA across all platforms.

Each site and landing page was designed and developed with a focus on component-based iterative design. In order to maintain consistency across all of the College of Engineering's specialties, we designed and developed an exhaustive set of components that could be repeated and branded across each individual site.

Following a sprint week model, I worked directly with their team to build the most important features first and begin testing the application process with real users and start meeting real needs.
