The Client : UA College of Engineering

Technologies Used : Wordpress / PHP / Photoshop

/ 01 Project context

As one of the premier engineering schools in the United States, the University of Arizona (UA) attracts prospective students from across the country and around the globe. Following a recent schoolwide rebrand, UA's College of Engineering needed an overhaul of its existing website presence in order to reflect a new set of branding guidelines.

I used this as an opportunity to refresh the aesthetics of their websites and improve a noticeably cumbersome online admissions process.

/ 01 The making of

Establishing a successful design system.

I worked closely with members of UA's marketing team to chose which brand updates were to take priority during the first phase of the relaunch.

We focused on updating the color palette and type stack, as well as developing a new lead capture process, as these would make the biggest impact upfront and help represent the College of Engineering within the bigger UA brand.

Web Color Palette

Evolution of a component library

Drawing inspiration from the natural landscapes sourroundg its campus, the The UA brand was built from the ground up with the natural landscape surounding their campus as its focus. The stacked 'A' logomark and use of triangular shapes draws inspiration from a mountainanoue terrain and became the primary symbols for UA across all platforms.

Each site and landing page was designed and developed with a focus on component-based iterative design. In order to maintain consistency across all of the College of Engineering's specialties, we designed and developed an exhaustive set of components that could be repeated and branded across each individual site.
